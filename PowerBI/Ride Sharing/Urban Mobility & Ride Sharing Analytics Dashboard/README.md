# Urban Mobility & Ride Sharing Analytics Dashboard

## Domain
Mobility Analytics | Ride Sharing Operations

## Overview
This dashboard analyzes ride-sharing performance across revenue, trip completion, cancellations, surge pricing, driver productivity, and customer behavior. The focus is on operational efficiency and monetization across cities, vehicles, time buckets, and user segments.

## Business Objective
- Track revenue and trip growth
- Monitor trip completion and cancellation behavior
- Optimize surge pricing and peak-hour strategy
- Evaluate driver productivity and earnings
- Understand customer contribution and retention

## Dataset
- Source: Public ride-sharing dataset
- Data Type: Transactional ride data
- Scope: Multi-month, multi-city operations

## Key Metrics
- Total Revenue: ₹1.74M
- Total Trips: 10K
- Completed Trips Rate: 89.78%
- Cancellation Rate: 10.22%
- Driver Earnings (Total): ₹1.25M
- Avg Fare (Completed): ₹194.05
- Avg Trip Duration: 28.66 mins
- Avg Wait Time: 6.16 mins
- Trips per Driver: 17.96
- Distinct Customers: ~2K
- NPS (Proxy): 0.76

## Analysis Performed
- Revenue and trips trend analysis (monthly & rolling)
- City-wise and hour-wise demand distribution
- Surge vs non-surge revenue comparison
- Peak vs off-peak revenue analysis
- Vehicle-type efficiency and duration analysis
- Driver earnings vs rating and utilization
- Customer segment revenue and frequency analysis
- Cancellation reason and driver/no-show analysis

## Key Insights
- ~90% trip completion indicates stable operations, but cancellations remain a revenue leakage.
- Peak hours contribute disproportionately higher revenue due to surge pricing.
- Bengaluru consistently leads in trips and surge impact.
- Bike and Mini vehicles show better trip efficiency and lower wait times.
- Higher surge improves revenue but slightly increases cancellations.
- Top drivers earn significantly more with higher utilization, not longer hours.
- Retail customers dominate volume; corporate users contribute higher fare value.
